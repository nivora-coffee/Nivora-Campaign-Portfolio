01 · Context

What I was hired to do!

Nivora Coffee Company is a new D2C brand selling South Indian filter coffee powder on Amazon India. They had a product and no digital presence. I came in to build their Meta Ads infrastructure from scratch — with zero prior pixel data, no retargeting audience, and no brand recognition to lean on.

My Role
End-to-end campaign management — strategy, audience design, copywriting, funnel architecture, pixel setup, creative testing, and post-campaign analysis.
Starting Point
No brand recognition. No existing pixel data. No retargeting audience. Every rupee spent was on cold traffic only.
Campaign Objective
Not to maximize sales on this run — to validate whether the funnel worked, which audiences responded, and which creatives performed before scaling.
The Core Constraint
No Amazon Brand Registry access, which meant no native pixel or attribution tools. This single constraint shaped every technical decision I made.

03 · Audience Strategy

Two audiences. Two distinct strategies.

I didn't run one generic coffee ad. I identified two psychographically distinct segments with different purchase barriers and built separate positioning for each — because the same message wouldn't work for both.

Ad Set 1
Young Adults · 19–25
Convenience-first mindset. Daily instant coffee users who don't fully understand what ground coffee is or why it would matter to them.
Barriers I had to overcome
Perceived effort — ground coffee feels complicated vs. instant
Awareness gap — many don't know the difference between ground and instant
My positioning strategy
Simplified the perception: it's an upgrade, not a new brewing ritual
Problem → Solution framing: instant is repetitive / Nivora is easy and better
📦 Freshly Ground Arabica/Robusta
Ad Set 2
Adults · 30–45
Already familiar with filter coffee taste. They use instant at home but know filter coffee is superior — they just haven't made the switch.
Barriers I had to overcome
Habit inertia — instant is the routine, switching feels unnecessary
Passive acceptance — they tolerate instant even though they prefer filter
My positioning strategy
Highlighted the gap: you know filter is better, so why not at home?
Framed as daily upgrade — not a lifestyle change or extra effort
☕ South Indian Filter Coffee Powder

06 · Thought Process

Why I made the decisions I made.

The results matter, but the decisions behind them matter more. This is how I think about running a campaign — and where most of the real work happens.

1
I ran a Sales objective — not a Traffic campaign
Optimised for ViewContent events, not the cheapest clicks
A standard Traffic campaign would have generated more clicks at a lower CPC and looked better on paper. I deliberately avoided it. Traffic campaigns optimise for the cheapest clicks — which typically come from low-intent users who immediately bounce. I structured this as a Sales objective with ViewContent as the conversion event, which teaches Meta's algorithm to find people likely to land on the bridge page and actually engage with the product. Lower volume, stronger signal. That's the trade I made — and the 86% bridge rate is the payoff.
Campaign Strategy
2
I built the bridge page before running a single ad
The funnel would have been a black box without it
Without Amazon Brand Registry, pixel placement on Amazon is impossible. Running ads directly to Amazon would have given us money in, zero data out. I built a custom landing page on GitHub Pages and integrated the Meta Pixel + CAPI before launch, routing users through it on the way to Amazon. This gave us PageView and ViewContent tracking, a controlled product story before the purchase decision, and the ability to build a retargeting audience for the next campaign. Without the bridge page, none of that data would exist.
Technical Architecture
3
I cut two creatives on Day 6, not Day 14
Knowing when to stop is as important as knowing when to scale
Creative 3 had spent ₹1.89 with 343 reach and zero clicks. Creative 4 had spent ₹1.34 with 239 reach and one click. The signal was clear early. I paused both immediately and redirected the full budget to the two performing creatives, which went on to reach 120K–149K people respectively. Waiting for the full test window would have been budget burn with no additional learning. Early clarity is better than delayed evidence.
Data Decision
4
I kept both audience segments despite similar performance
Similar results don't mean collapse into one — they mean dual-market fit
Both ad sets performed similarly across CTR, CPC, and ViewContent volume. A short-sighted read would be to pick one and drop the other. My interpretation: both audiences are genuinely interested in the product, which means Nivora has a dual-market fit. The right next step is not consolidation — it's to write separate, sharpened creative messaging for each segment so they're spoken to in their own language. Efficiency will follow once the messaging is segment-specific.

07 · Key Learnings

What this campaign confirmed.

Beyond the metrics — the strategic insights that will shape Nivora's next campaign and any brand I work with at this stage.

📱
Instagram Reels is the channel
96%+ of all clicks and ViewContent events came from Instagram Reels. Facebook got spend but minimal results. Future campaigns should go Instagram Reels-first — or Instagram-only — for maximum efficiency from day one.
⏳
Don't reset during the learning phase
CPM halved between Week 1 and Week 2 with zero campaign changes. The algorithm did the work. Every reset restarts that process from zero. I recommend a 7-day minimum hands-off window as a standard in every campaign going forward.
🎯
Attribution is the next problem to solve
Amazon's wall means purchase data stays invisible. This campaign likely drove more conversions than were reported. Moving to Shopify — or implementing Amazon Attribution links — would close the loop and unlock real ROAS tracking for scaling decisions.
📊
The market exists. This is not theory.
Cold traffic, zero social proof, zero retargeting, zero brand recognition — and a 1.75% CTR above industry benchmarks. Demand is there. The next campaign is about attribution and efficiency, not validation. The system works.
